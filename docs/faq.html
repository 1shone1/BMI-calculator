BMI Calculator FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions about BMI Calculation and Usage

What is BMI?
BMI (Body Mass Index) is a simple measurement using your weight and height to work out if your weight is healthy. The BMI calculation divides an adult's weight in kilograms by their height in meters squared.
How is BMI calculated?
BMI is calculated using the formula: BMI = weight (kg) ÷ height² (m²). For example, if you weigh 70kg and are 1.75m tall, your BMI would be 70 ÷ (1.75 × 1.75) = 22.9.
What are the BMI categories?
The standard BMI categories for adults are:
• Underweight: Below 18.5
• Normal weight: 18.5 to 24.9
• Overweight: 25 to 29.9
• Obese: 30 or greater
Is BMI accurate for everyone?
BMI is not a perfect measure as it doesn't account for factors like muscle mass, bone density, age, sex, ethnicity, and body composition. Athletes or muscular individuals might have a high BMI but not be overweight. It's best to use BMI as one of several indicators of health.
How often should I check my BMI?
It's recommended to check your BMI whenever there are significant changes in your weight or every 6-12 months as part of your regular health check-up. However, rapid or frequent changes in BMI should be discussed with a healthcare provider.
Can children use this BMI calculator?
This calculator is designed for adults. Children and teens should use a specific BMI calculator that takes into account their age and gender, as their body composition changes significantly during development. Please consult a pediatrician for children's BMI assessment.
