What is BMI?

BMI, or Body Mass Index, is a measure that uses height and weight to assess whether a person has a healthy body weight for their height. It is a useful tool for identifying whether individuals are underweight, at a healthy weight, overweight, or obese.

Importance of BMI

  • Helps assess health risks associated with weight
  • Provides a quick screening tool for weight categories
  • Can be an initial step in determining appropriate health interventions
  • Helps track weight management progress

Risks of Being Overweight or Underweight

Overweight Risks:

  • Heart disease and stroke
  • Type 2 diabetes
  • High blood pressure
  • Joint problems
  • Sleep apnea
  • Certain types of cancer

Underweight Risks:

  • Nutritional deficiencies
  • Weakened immune system
  • Osteoporosis
  • Fertility issues
  • Developmental issues in children
